Domestic Violence Data Illustrates Citywide Epidemic

Data from city officials proves that domestic violence not isolated to specific parts of the city

By: Parker E. Quinlan

While domestic violence has always been an issue, data from city officials reveals the scope of the problem across New York City. (Photo via Unsplash.com)

At a Dec 5 press conference to discuss crime statistics, Mayor Bill de Blasio announced that situations of domestic abuse account for nearly 20% of all murders in the city.

Part of a larger surge of domestic violence in New York, data from city officials reveals that reports of domestic violence continues to persist across the five boroughs, even when accounting for population.

Throughout the city, incidents of domestic violence remain high, even as city officials attempt to raise awareness about the issue, and resources which exist for victims.

The de Blasio administration has been working to reduce the overall number of domestic violence incidents, though data from the Mayor’s Office to End Domestic and Gender-Based Violence indicates the problem is still persistent in many parts of the city.

Though primarily concentrated in the Bronx and eastern Brooklyn, the rate of domestic violence incidence reports – a form filed by the NYPD to indicate a domestic incident may have taken place – is also high in northern Staten Island.

The city has a requires employee training on sexual assualt and training, though advocates are concerned that the training does not go far enough, and should be expanded.

Stephanie Nilva, Executive Director of Day One, a nonprofit which provides trainings on sexual abuse and domestic violence, expressed some skepticism about whether the 45-minute city mandiated training was adequate.

“Our trainings can range from one hour to one day depending on the needs of the organization,” said Nilva, “It is very unlikely that you will be able to cover everything in a 45-minute training.”
